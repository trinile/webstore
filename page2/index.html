Patagonia: Landmarks

Glacier Moreno

Location:

Parque Nacionales Los Glaciares, southwest Santa Cruz Province, Argentina

About:

Watch glaciers at this stunning centerpiece of the southern Parque Nacional Los Glaciares. This glacier, measures 30km long, 5km wide, and 6 m high. It is exceptional for its constant advance - it creeps forward 2m a day, caussing building-sized icebergs to calve from its face. Some even kayak around the glacier into caves.

Notable features

  • Length: 19 mi (30 km)
  • Area: 250 km2 ice formation

Fauna

  • Choique aka Leser Rhea
  • Guanacos

Mt Fitz Roy

Location:

Southern Patagonian Ice Field, on border between Argentina and Chile; near El Chalten Village

About:

Located in the northern part of Parque Nacional Los Glaciares, Mt. Fitz Roy Range's impressive snow-covered peak attracts world class climbers. Despite an average height of 11,290ft, it is considered among the most technically challenging mountains to climb due to its sheer granite faces that require long stretches of difficult technical climbing. Weather can also be inclement and treacherous. It and El Chalten (village) are considered the trekking capital of Argentina."

Notable Features

  • Elevation: 11073 ft.
  • Prominence: 6,401'

Fauna

  • Guanacos
  • Flamingos
  • Pumas
  • Nandu

Best Times to Visit

December through February (sumer months)
