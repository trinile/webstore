Patagonia: National Parks

Parques Nacionales Los Glaciares

Location:

Austral Andes, Argentina.

About:

This park shows a scenario of mountains, lakes and woods, including a large portion of the Andes practically under ice and snow to the west, and the arid Patagonian steppe to the east. Its name refer to the glaciers that are born on the Ice Caps - the largest continental ice extension after Anctartica- which occupies almost half its area. Also known as Patagonic Continental Ice, creates 47 big glaciers, 13 of which flow to the Atlantic. There are also more than 200 smaller glaciers, unconnected to the Ice Caps.

Notable Landmarks

  • Glacier Moreno
  • Mount Fitz Roy

Fauna

  • Choique aka Leser Rhea
  • Guanacos

Torres Del Paine National Park

Location:

Extreme Southern region of Patogonian Chile

About:

The Torres del Paine (Spanish for "Towers of Paine", "Paine" being the old indigenous name for the colour blue), three immense rock towers give the park its name.

Features mountains, lakes, and glaciers.

Notable Landmarks

  • Torres Del Paine
  • Cuernos Del Paine
  • Grey Glacier
  • Lago Grey
  • Lago Peho
  • Lago Nordenskjold
  • Lago Sarmiento

Fauna

  • Guanacos
  • Flamingos
  • Pumas
  • Nandu

Best Times to Visit

November to April (spring and summer in southern himsphere)

Patagonia: Landmarks

Glacier Moreno

Location:

Parque Nacionales Los Glaciares, southwest Santa Cruz Province, Argentina

About:

Watch glaciers at this stunning centerpiece of the southern Parque Nacional Los Glaciares. This glacier, measures 30km long, 5km wide, and 6 m high. It is exceptional for its constant advance - it creeps forward 2m a day, caussing building-sized icebergs to calve from its face. Some even kayak around the glacier into caves.

Notable features

  • Length: 19 mi (30 km)
  • Area: 250 km2 ice formation

Fauna

  • Choique aka Leser Rhea
  • Guanacos

Mt Fitz Roy

Location:

Southern Patagonian Ice Field, on border between Argentina and Chile; near El Chalten Village

About:

Located in the northern part of Parque Nacional Los Glaciares, Mt. Fitz Roy Range's impressive snow-covered peak attracts world class climbers. Despite an average height of 11,290ft, it is considered among the most technically challenging mountains to climb due to its sheer granite faces that require long stretches of difficult technical climbing. Weather can also be inclement and treacherous. It and El Chalten (village) are considered the trekking capital of Argentina."

Notable Features

  • Elevation: 11073 ft.
  • Prominence: 6,401'

Fauna

  • Guanacos
  • Flamingos
  • Pumas
  • Nandu

Best Times to Visit

December through February (summer months)
